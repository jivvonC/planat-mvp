# 04_EXPERIENCE_PRINCIPLES.md

# Planat

### Experience Principles

Version 1.0

---

# Purpose

This document defines the core experiences that Planat should deliver.

These principles guide every interaction, animation, feature, and user flow.

Whenever a new feature is designed, it should support one or more of these experiences.

If a feature does not reinforce these principles, reconsider the design.

---

# Experience 01

## Caring should feel easier than recording.

### Why

User research showed that people understand the value of keeping plant records.

However, most users stop recording because it feels repetitive and burdensome.

The problem is not motivation.

The problem is friction.

---

### Experience Goal

Users should never feel like they are documenting their plants.

They should simply feel like they are caring for them.

The system quietly creates meaningful records in the background.

---

### Design Principles

- Most care actions should require only one tap.
- Recording should never interrupt the experience.
- Notes and photos are optional.
- The Calendar is built automatically.

---

### Examples

💧 Tap "Water"

↓

Watering animation

↓

Small feedback message

↓

Calendar updates automatically

No additional recording process.

---

# Experience 02

## Observation comes before action.

### Why

Beginners often know _what_ to do but not _what_ to observe.

Helping users notice small changes builds confidence and understanding.

---

### Experience Goal

Before suggesting solutions,

help users look at their plants.

Curiosity should come before instruction.

---

### Design Principles

- Ask simple questions.
- Guide observation step by step.
- Never overwhelm users with information.
- Let users discover changes naturally.

---

### Example

Instead of

"Your plant has root rot."

Guide users through

Leaf color

↓

Leaf position

↓

Soil condition

↓

Lighting

↓

Diagnosis

# Experience 03

## Every interaction becomes part of the journey.

### Why

People become attached to plants through repeated moments of care.

Small interactions matter more than occasional large efforts.

---

### Experience Goal

Every watering,

every mission,

every observation

should become a memory.

Users should feel that time spent with their plants is meaningful.

---

### Design Principles

- Automatically save care history.
- Visualize growth over time.
- Show accumulated moments instead of isolated actions.

---

### Example

Today's Mission

↓

Observation

↓

Watering

↓

Automatically added to Calendar

↓

Visible in future AI diagnosis

---

# Experience 04

## Encourage, never pressure.

### Why

People stop using plant care apps when they begin feeling like productivity tools.

Planat should reduce anxiety,

not increase it.

---

### Experience Goal

Users should feel welcomed every time they open the app.

Even if they haven't cared for their plants recently.

---

### Design Principles

- Never create guilt.
- Never punish missed actions.
- Celebrate small successes.
- Use positive language.

---

### Example

Instead of

"You missed watering."

Use

"오늘은 흙 상태를 함께 살펴볼까요?"

---

# Experience 05

## AI should teach, not replace observation.

### Why

The purpose of AI is not simply to generate answers.

Its role is to help users become more confident plant owners.

---

### Experience Goal

Users should gradually learn how to observe plants themselves.

Every diagnosis should improve future understanding.

---

### Design Principles

- Ask one question at a time.
- Explain why questions matter.
- Explain why the diagnosis was made.
- Suggest observation for next time.

---

### Example

"잎 끝이 갈색인 이유는 과습 때문일 가능성이 높아요."

↓

"다음에는 흙이 충분히 말랐는지도 함께 확인해보세요."

---

# Experience 06

## Caring should feel rewarding.

### Why

Users are more likely to continue caring for plants when interactions feel emotionally satisfying.

Even very small actions deserve positive reinforcement.

---

### Experience Goal

Users should leave the app feeling

"I did something good today."

---

### Design Principles

- Celebrate small actions.
- Use gentle animations.
- Use positive feedback.
- Keep interactions lightweight.

---

### Example

Water Button

↓

Water droplet animation

↓

Plant reacts slightly

↓

"오늘도 식물과 좋은 시간을 보냈어요."

---

# Experience 07

## The Calendar is a memory, not a checklist.

### Why

Traditional calendars emphasize completed tasks.

Planat's Calendar should emphasize shared experiences.

---

### Experience Goal

Looking at the Calendar should remind users

how their plants have grown,

not how many tasks they completed.

---

### Design Principles

- Show moments rather than tasks.
- Include observations.
- Include optional notes and photos.
- Make growth visually recognizable.

---

### Example

June 24

💧 물을 주었어요

🌱 새 잎이 올라왔어요

📷 오늘의 모습을 남겼어요

📝 "새 잎이 지난주보다 훨씬 커졌어요."

---

# Experience 08

## Every screen should feel calm.

### Why

Plant care is often associated with healing and relaxation.

The interface should reinforce that feeling.

---

### Experience Goal

Users should slow down while using the app.

The interface should never feel rushed.

---

### Design Principles

- Comfortable spacing.
- Soft transitions.
- Minimal information.
- One primary action per screen.
- Generous white space.

---

# Experience Checklist

Whenever designing a new feature,

ask the following questions.

□ Does this encourage observation?

□ Can this interaction be simplified?

□ Does recording happen naturally?

□ Does the interface reduce pressure?

□ Does this strengthen emotional attachment?

□ Does the Calendar remember this moment?

□ Would a beginner immediately understand what to do?

□ Does this interaction feel warm?

---

# Final Principle

Users should never feel that they are managing plants.

They should feel that they are spending a little time with something alive.

Everything else happens quietly in the background.

**Users care.**

**Planat remembers.**
